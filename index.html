Info about HIV/AIDS

About HIV/AIDS

If we have HIV our immune system are weak. Then it is easy to get other infections. These are called opportunist infections. STI, pneumonia and TB are examples of opportunistic infection.Human immunodeficiency virus is an infection that attacks the body's immune system. Acquired immunodeficiency syndrome is the most advanced stage of the disease. HIV targets the body's white blood cells, weakening the immune system.

Treatment Action Campaign

The Treatment Action Campaignis a large human rights advocacy organization. It provides people living with HIV their families and caregivers with accurate information about life saving medicine and treatment. The TAC advocates for the human rights of people living with HIV and AIDS.

Important facts

Treatment for HIV and AIDS varies depending on the health of the HIV-positive person,or on the progression of AIDS. You can live a long and reasonable healthy life if you look after yourself. Regularly go for HIV councelling and testing know your status. Antiretrovirals do not cure the disease however,they help you cope with the disease.You need to start taking ARVs when your CD counts is 200 or less.

Advice

Be safe

  • Always be positve and healthy
  • Be on treatment
  • Go for counselling regularly
  • Know your status

Support someone with HIV/AIDS

  • Listen to their needs.
  • Learn about HIV.
  • Encourage them to start HIV treatment as soon as possible.
  • Support medication adhernce
